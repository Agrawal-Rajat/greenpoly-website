Frequently Asked Questions

Bioplastics are materials made from renewable plant sources like corn starch, sugarcane, or cassava. If a product contains at least 25% plant-derived materials, it qualifies as a bioplastic. Our packaging solutions use these natural materials to minimize environmental harm.

Biodegradable products naturally break down over time with the help of microbes, but compostable materials go a step further — they break down into non-toxic components under specific conditions and leave no harmful residue behind. All our products meet compostable standards.
